## How Time Stop Damage Actually Works

Most players treat Time Stop as “free damage time,” but that’s incomplete. The game calculates your burst in layers. If you don’t stack layers in the right order, your result is weaker than expected.

| Layer | What it affects | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Base Attack/Skill Coeff** | Raw hit value | Foundation of every burst sequence |
| **Element/Type Bonus** | Damage family scaling | Big gain if your stop-burst skill matches element buffs |
| **Crit Rate/Crit Damage** | Multiplicative spike | Best return when time stop guarantees or boosts crit consistency |
| **Defense Ignore / Penetration** | Effective damage dealt | Essential against late-game elites and abyss bosses |
| **Execution/Threshold Effects** | Finish damage | Turns “almost kill” into clean one-cycle kills |

In 2026, top players prioritize **reliable burst floors** over lucky peaks. That means your minimum burst in Time Stop should still clear your target breakpoint (boss phase break, elite deletion, or PvP opener).

## Common Mistakes That Kill Time Stop DPS

Even strong accounts throw away damage due to sequencing errors.

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Casting burst before debuff | You lose multiplicative value | Always mark first unless opener race matters |
| Overcapping cooldown | More windows, weaker windows | Reallocate into penetration/crit |
| Crit damage with low crit rate | Spiky, inconsistent results | Stabilize crit rate before scaling crit damage |
| Using Time Stop on weak waves | Wasted cycle timing | Save for elite clusters or priority targets |
| Ignoring animation lock | Missed final hit before stop ends | Practice cast-cancel and timing rhythm |

A useful benchmark: if your full combo cannot remove an elite in one Time Stop cycle, your build needs stat reshuffling, not just better execution.
