## Starting Your Journey in Anime Apocalypse

When you first load into the game, the sheer amount of options can be overwhelming. The primary goal for any new player is to establish a reliable source of experience (XP) and currency. Unlike other titles where the campaign is the focus, the heart of this experience lies in the Infinite Mode. This mode allows you to face endless waves of enemies, scaling in difficulty but also in the quality of the rewards.

### The First 60 Minutes
Initially, your focus should be on familiarizing yourself with the movement mechanics and basic character abilities. You will start with a basic roster, but your objective is to quickly move toward unlocking higher-tier characters. Always keep an eye on the Daily Quests tab, as these offer a consistent stream of coins for very little effort.

> **💡 Tip:** Do not ignore the Daily Quests. Even if you only have 10 minutes to play, completing these tasks provides the baseline currency needed for essential character upgrades.

## Character Tier List: Who to Prioritize

Selecting the right character is the most critical decision in this **Anime Apocalypse beginner guide**. While many players chase after the rarest "Secret" or "Legendary" units, some "Epic" characters punch significantly above their weight class.

| Character | Rarity | Tier | Why They Are Good |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sasuke** | Epic | **S-Tier** | Incredible AOE damage and crowd control early on. |
| **Gojo** | Legendary | **S-Tier** | Unmatched survivability and high burst damage. |
| **Luffy** | Rare | **B-Tier** | Solid starter for single-target damage but falls off late. |
| **Zoro** | Epic | **A-Tier** | Fast attack speed, great for clearing trash mobs. |

It is a common mistake for beginners to overlook **Sasuke**. Despite being an Epic rarity, his ability kit is perfectly suited for the Infinite Mode meta. He can clear large groups of zombies quickly, which accelerates your farming speed and keeps you safe from being cornered.

## Progression Systems and Battle Pass

To truly excel, you must look beyond just killing zombies. The game features several underlying systems that provide passive and active boosts to your account.

### Achievements and Battle Pass
The Achievement system is a massive source of XP. By simply playing the game, you will unlock milestones that grant you both standard XP and Battle Pass XP. The Battle Pass is particularly important for free-to-play players, as it contains:
*   **Trait Rerolls:** Essential for perfecting your character's stats.
*   **Exclusive Cosmetics:** Visual upgrades that show off your progress.
*   **Currency Boosts:** Temporary items that increase your coin gain.

### The Conversion and Crafting Tab
If you find yourself with an inventory full of items you don't recognize or need, head over to the **Convert** menu. This allows you to break down unwanted materials into components required for high-tier crafting. This is especially useful for players looking to customize their appearance through the cosmetic crafting system without spending real-world money.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when converting items. Some materials, like Cursed Fingers, are much harder to find later on. Always check if a character evolution requires the material before breaking it down.

## Advanced Combat Tactics for Beginners

Surviving past Wave 50 in Shibuya requires more than just high stats. You need to understand enemy pathing and ability cooldown management.

1.  **Kiting the Horde:** Never stand still. Use the map's geometry to keep the zombies in a tight group (a "death ball") so your AOE abilities hit as many targets as possible.
2.  **Resource Cycling:** Coordinate your big cooldowns. If you are playing in a team, ensure everyone isn't using their ultimate abilities at the same time.
3.  **Prioritize Special Zombies:** Some enemies have ranged attacks or speed boosts. Identify these early and eliminate them before they can disrupt your movement.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the best map for a beginner to start farming?**

A: The best map for farming is Shibuya in Infinite Mode. It offers a great balance of enemy density and navigation, making it ideal for both solo players and teams looking to gain XP and coins quickly.

**Q: Is Sasuke actually good even though he isn't a Legendary character?**

A: Yes, Sasuke is widely considered S-tier for beginners. His AOE (Area of Effect) abilities make him one of the best characters for clearing waves in Infinite Mode, often outperforming higher rarity characters in farming efficiency.

**Q: How do I get more Trait Rerolls without spending Robux?**

A: The most reliable way to get Trait Rerolls is through the Battle Pass. By completing achievements and daily quests, you can level up your Battle Pass and earn these rerolls as free rewards.

**Q: What should I do with the extra items like Screws and Leaves?**

A: These items are primarily used in the Crafting and Cosmetics section. If you don't care about cosmetics, you can use the "Convert" function to turn unneeded materials into resources that help with character progression.
