## The Infinite Mode Meta: Shibuya Dominance

When it comes to pure resource accumulation, Infinite Mode stands head and shoulders above standard missions. Specifically, the Shibuya map has emerged as the premier location for high-tier farming. The layout of Shibuya allows for better crowd control and more predictable zombie pathing, making it ideal for both solo players and coordinated teams.

While the game offers various environments, Shibuya provides the best density of enemies relative to the time spent per wave. If you are looking to maximize your efficiency, you should prioritize this map above all others. You can choose to tackle this solo if your units are sufficiently leveled, but teaming up often allows for faster wave clears, which indirectly increases your hourly resource rate.

## Optimizing Your Anime Apocalypse Farming Guide Strategy

### Achievements and Battle Pass Synergy

One of the biggest mistakes new players make is ignoring the Achievement menu. Achievements provide a significant boost to your base XP and, more importantly, Battle Pass XP. The Battle Pass is a goldmine for essential items like trait rerolls, which can drastically change the power level of your units.

1.  **Daily Quests:** Complete these every single day. They offer a steady stream of coins that act as the backbone of your economy.
2.  **Achievement Hunting:** Focus on achievements that overlap with your farming. If an achievement requires killing 1,000 zombies, Infinite Shibuya is the place to do it.
3.  **Battle Pass Progression:** Use the items gained from the Battle Pass to optimize your units' traits. A single "S-tier" trait can make a unit twice as effective in Infinite mode.

## Advanced Tactics for Solo Players

If you prefer to play solo, your strategy must shift toward sustainability. In a team, you can rely on others to cover lanes, but in solo play, your unit placement is critical. Ensure that your AoE units like Sasuke are placed at "choke points" where zombies are forced to cluster. This maximizes the damage dealt per attack and ensures that no stragglers reach the end of the map.

Furthermore, keep an eye on your unit upgrades during the match. It is often better to have one fully upgraded S-tier unit than four low-level units. The power scaling in the later waves of Infinite is aggressive, and "stat-checking" the zombies with a high-level Sasuke is the most reliable way to reach the 20-minute mark consistently.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest way to get coins in Anime Apocalypse?**

A: The fastest method is currently running the Shibuya map on Infinite mode. A 20-minute run can yield upwards of 250,000 coins if you have efficient wave-clear units like Sasuke.

**Q: Why is Sasuke considered S-tier if he is only an Epic?**

A: Sasuke’s abilities provide superior Area of Effect (AoE) damage, which is essential for clearing the massive hordes found in Infinite mode. His accessibility makes him the best "bang for your buck" unit for any **Anime Apocalypse farming guide** enthusiast.

**Q: What should I do with the Cursed Fingers I farm?**

A: Cursed Fingers are a vital crafting ingredient. They are primarily used for evolving units and crafting high-level gear. Do not sell them; save them for the late-game evolution process.

**Q: Can I farm cosmetics and power-up items at the same time?**

A: Yes. Infinite mode drops a variety of items including leaves and screws (for cosmetics) alongside coins and fingers (for power). This makes it the most time-efficient activity in the game.
